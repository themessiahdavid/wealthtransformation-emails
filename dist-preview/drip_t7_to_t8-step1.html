I want to tell you about a man named Tom.

Tom managed a dollar store in Fairbanks, Alaska, in a year I'm not going to date because the year doesn't matter. What matters is who I was that year.

I was deep in what I now call the standard playbook. Wandering, listing, calling, three-waying. Two years of full effort. About a thousand dollars a month to show for it.

Tom lived in the back of his store. Literally. He had hollowed out a walk-in cooler, pulled the cooling unit, dropped a mattress in. That was his apartment. He'd been doing it for years. He had no business interest, no upside, nothing he was angling for. He wasn't a prospect. He was a man behind a register who occasionally let me sit in his cave and warm up after the wandering.

One Tuesday at 11:14pm, in the cave, I ran a three-way call with a grandmother on my list. The grandmother said yes. Five minutes. I hung up.

I felt the producer's high. The little dopamine pop. Look at me. Closed one.

Tom looked at me and said the line.

I would not do what you just did for two hundred thousand dollars a month.

Tom wasn't angling for anything. Tom wasn't selling me a course. Tom was a man who lived in a fridge, looking at a man who'd just routed his Tuesday night through an eleven-o'clock grandmother call, and naming what he saw.

He was right.

The trap wasn't Tom's life. The trap was the playbook running on me. The playbook that pretended a Tuesday at 11:14pm was a victory. The playbook that fed me five-minute dopamine hits while my actual life — the dream from twenty-two, sleep, the people I loved — drifted out the back of the building one quiet pull at a time.

The man living in a fridge was the witness. I was the one in the trap.

Producer Transformation is teaching you to be a producer. Necessary work. You can't multiply something that doesn't produce.

But the thing you're going to feel inside the next ninety days — once the producing starts working — is the same trap I was in the night Tom said the line.

You'll start hitting your number. And your number will require you. Every hour of it. Every call. Every DM. Every three-way. Every almost-leader who asks you for help they could give themselves.

You'll start to feel the producer's high and Tom's diagnostic at the same time. The dopamine that says finally. The body that says this can't be the answer.

That's the moment Team Transformation exists for. Not the recruiting moment. The moment after the recruiting works.

The course teaches an architecture that puts the producing inside a system instead of inside you. Identity, not activity. When you take the lever from the activity level to the identity level, your team's behavior changes downstream of who you've become. You don't have to take the eleven-o'clock call. The architecture takes it.

You're not there yet. You're still in the producing chapter. Take the time you need.

But when you start to feel Tom in the back of your own week — when the next eleven-o'clock call starts to feel like the trap instead of the win — you'll know what email to come back to.

— David
