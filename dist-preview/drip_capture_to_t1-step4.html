The voice is wrong. The hooks aren't dying because they're uninteresting. They're dying because they're shaped wrong.

I want you to be able to write your first hook tonight, with or without the playbook. So I'm pulling one of the seven hook archetypes out of the toolkit and giving it to you here, in usable form, ungated.

The archetype is called the Reversal.

It works like this. Most things people believe about your topic point in one direction. The Reversal opens by stating the opposite — calmly, specifically, and with enough detail that the viewer pauses to check whether you mean it.

The reason it works isn't psychological. It's structural. Human attention moves toward a contradiction it cannot immediately resolve. The Reversal places that contradiction in the first three seconds, and the rest of your short becomes the resolution.

Here's the template.

"Most [common bond] think [common belief]. The opposite is closer to true. Here's the [number]-second version of why."

Three worked examples, in three different niches, so you can see how the structure travels.

Real estate: "Most agents think the listing is what sells the house. The buyer's nervous system is what sells the house, and 80% of that decision happens before they walk in the front door."

Strength training: "Most lifters think the failure set is where strength is built. The set before the failure set is where strength is built. The failure set is where confidence is built — and you can't trade them."

Personal finance: "Most people think the budget makes you save. The budget makes you sad. What makes you save is one automated transfer at midnight on the first of the month, before you've made a single discretionary decision."

Read those three again. Notice the shape. The first sentence is the reversal — the contradiction is set in nine to fourteen words. The second sentence is the new claim. The third sentence is either the consequence or the mechanism. That is the entire hook.

Run this template tonight. Pick your topic. Write the most defensible piece of conventional wisdom in your space. Reverse it in nine to fourteen words. Add the new claim. Add the consequence. That's a Hook beat. Yours.
